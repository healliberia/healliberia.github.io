About Us

Holistic Education Advocating Leadership (HEAL) is a 501(c) 3 non-profit initiative, tackling the epidemic of rape and sexual assault in Liberia, West Africa and communities alike. Among other things, HEAL wishes to raise awareness about the alarming high number of rape against children under the age of twelve, educate Liberians and communities alike on the long and short term effects of rape and sexual assault, advocate for victims of rape and sexual assault, and make DNA forensics available in order to provide rape kits, and to seek justice for rape victims. HEAL ultimately wants to open a forensics laboratory in Liberia with the sole purpose of providing DNA analysis that will help bring rape perpetrators to justice.

Mawata Kamara - HEAL Founder and Executive Director

Mawata was born and raised in Liberia, West Africa. She remembers packing for what was supposed to be a one-week trip. After hiding all her dolls and favorite things in different locations of the house, she climbed into the back of a pick-up truck that too her and her family to neighboring Guinea. A week turned into months, months into years, and years into a journey that she could never have imagined.

Her future was shaped by the nurses and personnel who worked for the United Nations in Liberia and in the refugee camps in Sierra Leone. The teachers who challenged her to strive to do better. An unexpected recommendation for a scholarship that kept her in college. And her oldest sister, who to this day remains proof that a life of service is possible. It was through my interactions with each of these people, and more, that Mawata dreamed someday she would create an organization to help people.

After she attended the LeaderShape Institute in 2006 while studying at Temple University, her dream started to feel achievable. It was this profound educational experience that taught her that she could be the one who starts the ripple effect in the calm pond. She left inspired, with a vision and plan for HEAL in hand.

In April 2012, Mawata went to Liberia with a heavy heart but came back with a concrete purpose. After years of being emotionally paralyzed and distanced from my family by my secrets of sexual assault, she took an interest in the epidemic of rape.

During this trip, Mawata decided silence was no longer an option. She heard too often of victims of rape who were barely old enough to begin school, young victims whose lives and stories were similar to the lives of her relatives, and friends who endured similar tragedies. The effects of rape are everywhere in Liberia — on street corners, in restaurants, at the marketplace, written in local newspaper headlines, and broadcast through public service announcements.

It was quite clear to Mawata that her vision for HEAL would begin where her journey started - Liberia.

Projects

Safe Touch, Unsafe Touch

“Safe Touch, Unsafe Touch” is a culturally sensitive workshop designed by HEAL and the First Witness Child Advocacy Program of Minnesota. This workshop teaches children to diffrentiate pedophiliac interest versus friendliness. It teaches parents and children what to do should they become a victim of rape and/or sexual assault. It also teaches parents ways to recognize signs of abuse. The workshop incorporates appropriate actions needed to preserve DNA forensics in rape cases and resources to actively advocate for their children. This workshop is to be conducted at schools throughout Liberia, at community events within Liberia and within the Liberian community in the USA.

Training

HEAL founder, Mawata Kamara and Operations Director, Katherine Redd were both trained by First Witness Advocacy of Duluth on the principles of child advocacy and how the model used by First Advocacy center can be applied in Liberia and communities alike. HEAL hopes to take members of First Witness to Liberia to train professionals that are front-line in rape or abuse. In addition, HEAL is planning to partner with Sorenson Forensics to host a training in Liberia on how to handle evidence involving a rape case as well as how to use DNA results to prosecute rapists.

My Childhood is your future

Traditionally in Liberia and countries alike, there is an unspoken rule that children are meant to be seen and not heard. In fact, if a child is to speak without permission or speak while adults are conversing, that child is often label as disrespectful. Although this mentality has its significance within Liberian culture, it is often misused. This mentality is often the underlying cause of unjust silence amongst children; leaving them more vulnerable to crimes such as rape and violence. HEAL hosted its first annual peaceful march in Monrovia to encourage children to be their own advocates and remind them of the power of their voice. The day was dedicated to the children of Liberia. It was a chance for them to express themselves, be heard and PLAY!

Small Small Thing Documentary

HEAL partnered with the creators of "Small Small Thing" to show viewings of their award winning documentary. THe documentary tells the story of the rape and death of a nine years old girl, Olivia Zinnah. It also does an excellent job of highlighting the many social and judicial issues surrounding rape and sexual assault in Liberia. HEAL and the creators of the documentary hope to shine the light on this important issue.

Palava H.U.T.

Palava HUT is an open forum conducted in various locations in the community. This forum discusses rape and sexual assault and the effects it has on the community. The acronym H.U.T stands for Hearing, Understanding and Teaching. The goal of the Palava HUT is to allow community members to be informed, provide feedback, and create teachers within our communities.

Proof is in the DNA

HEAL partnered with Tru-Vision Clothing on a t-shirt campaign. The campaign includes images from members of the Liberian community stating how they feel about rape and sexual assault or why they support HEAL.

Community Outreach

HEAL takes advantage of multiple forums to engage the public. This includes appearances on radio shows, newspaper articles, and collaboration in community events such as the Miss Liberia, USA pageant and the Young Liberia Youth summit. HEAL founder, Mawata Kamara, hosts a monthly radio show on Radio Liberia.
